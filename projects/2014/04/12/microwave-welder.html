Microwave Welder

Apr 12, 2014

This weekend my friend and I decided to convert an old microwave into a spot welder. We were inspired by a video on YouTube on how to do it so we spent a weekend trying to make it a reality.

You will need:

  • An old microwave to take apart (or a brand new one that you dont really care about)
  • About 4 feet of the thickest wire you can find/afford at a hardware store (Note: the thicker the better)
  • Work gloves with NO HOLES IN THEM
  • Non synthetic clothing
  • Something that can reliably cut metal. This can be a hacksaw, plasma cutter, or in our case a reciprocating saw with the right type of blade.
  • A bench vice or a handful of c-clamps
  • Basic tools such as screwdrivers to take apart the microwave with

Step 0

Take apart the microwave. Inside you’ll find lots of cool, awesome high voltage parts but the one we are most interested in is the transformer which will look something like this:

Remove this part from the microwave. Make sure to wear your gloves while doing this and make sure that the microwave is unplugged.

Step 1

Now that your transformer is out, it’s time to learn a little bit about how these things work. Transformers are essentially devices that change the amount of current and voltage from a given source to fit the needs of a certain appliance. Normally, a microwave will take the current from the wall outlet and increase the voltage to 20,000 volts. For our purposes, we will instead modify the transformer to increase instead the amperage up to 2,000 A.

How does this melt metal? This happens because of the difference in resistance between the copper wire and the iron/steel piece of metal. Normally this delta is negligible but with a current this high, the difference is enough to cause the steel piece to melt.

Step 2

Now, you need to cut open the transformer and replace the secondary coil of it with one single winding of the thick wire you have. This wil reduce the voltage of the output and increase the current greatly. In order to cut open the transformer I suggest placing it in a vice and cutting along the welded seem on the transformer. This seem is pretty thin so a simple hacksaw should be fine but because I am lazy I used a reciprocating saw with a hacksaw blade. Be careful with this step and make sure not to not damage the primary coil (the coil with the thicker wire originally).

Step 3

Plug it in and melt stuff!!

Okay not really but you are basically done at this point. Remember to be mindful of danger in this project. Make sure to wear safety goggles and non-synthetic clothes.
